<p>this is called Operators Precedence, you can work with this by placing "(" around your formulas.</p>

<p>Say you plan to do </p>

<pre><code>(buy-Sell) * Sales Tax = Tax on Invoice
</code></pre>

<p>leave away the brackets and it would execute like this </p>

<pre><code>buy - (Sell * Sales Tax) = BAD Tax on Invoice
</code></pre>

<p>You can "google" Operators Precedence to see the operators precedence applied for a given language as it applies to more than * and /</p>

<p>Have a look at this <a href="https://www.tutorialspoint.com/csharp/csharp_operators_precedence.htm" rel="nofollow noreferrer">link</a> that goes and explains a bit in C#</p>
