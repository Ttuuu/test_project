<p>Your code is fine, but neither the question nor your answer have anything to do with threads. In order to run two separate threads, you need to create a <code>Thread</code> object with your runnable, rather than just calling <code>myRunnable.run()</code>. The first runnable returns to the scope of your <code>main()</code> method before the second is executed, i.e. they are sequential and exist in a single thread.</p>
