<p>Following from @Pimgd's suggestion about algorithmic optimizations, if you're finding an algorithm is too slow, finding a better one is usually the way to increasing the speed the most. </p>

<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dictionary_attack" rel="nofollow">Dictionary attacks</a> are much faster than iterating, although they require much more space for the speed that you gain. If you're looking to take into account the fact that passwords should be hashed, a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rainbow_table" rel="nofollow">rainbow table attack</a> is something else to look into.</p>
