<p>In LIKE patterns, the interpretation of characters between square brackets is more literal than normal.  What SQL dialect are you targeting?  See, for example, the <a href="http://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/ms179859.aspx" rel="nofollow">T-SQL documentation for <code>LIKE</code></a> (under <em>Using Wildcard Characters as Literals</em>).  <code>LIKE '5[%]'</code> should be translated as regex <code>'^5%$'</code>.</p>
